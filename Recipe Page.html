Pork Chop with Manchego Grids

Time: 45 minutes | Level: Medium

Ingredients

  • Pork Chop

    • 12-14 oz double-cut pork chop

  • Pork Marinade

    • 2 cup Brown Sugar
    • 4 tbsp each: Cumin, Ancho, Paprika, Salt, Garlic Powder, Oregano, Cayenne, Pepper

  • Apple vanilla Chutney

    • 1 fine diced onion
    • 6 apples cut into 1/3” wedges
    • 1 tbsp ginger
    • 2 cup granulated sugar
    • 1 cup apple cider
    • 1 cup apple cider
    • 1.5 cup apple cider vinegar
    • 2 tbsp vanillar
    • 2 parsnips small dice

  • Manchego Grits

    • 4 cup water
    • 1 cup cream
    • 1 cup manchego
    • 1/3 lb butter
    • ancho chilli powder

  • Kale

    • Deglaze pan with apple cider vinegar, add kale and tossork Rub

Step by Step

  1. Marinade the pork chop in spice blend for 30 minutes.


  2. While the pork chop marinades, take a medium saucepan, sweat the onions, ginger, and parsnips. When they begin to brown lightly, add your apple. Deglaze with vinegar, cook out the vinegar until the smell subsides. Finally, add sugar, vanilla and apple cider and cook on medium until the liquid has reduced to a syrupy consistency.


  3. Now your pork chop is ready to cook. Preheat the oven to 400º Heat a pan on medium high heat until the oil is about to smoke. Sear one side until dark brown, flip over and place in the oven.


  4. Bring cream and water to a boil and season aggressively with ancho chili and salt. Add your grits slowly while whisking. When the mixture begins to thicken, add your cheese until it reaches the desired consistency (liquid enough to pour, thick enough to hold its shape). Add butter to finish.


  5. After 10 minutes, remove the pork chop from pan and deglaze pan with apple cider. Add kale to the pan and wilt in the cider. Season and serve on the grits under the pork. Finally, top off your pork chop with the delicious apply chutney.
